Networking Overview

Computer networking is how devices—like computers, phones, and servers—connect and communicate with each other to share data and resources. Understanding networks is essential in today's digital world. Below is a quick look at the key parts of networking:

Networking Components

Networks are made up of several key components that help devices connect and communicate:

  • Routers: Direct data between different networks.
  • Switches: Connect devices within a network and manage traffic.
  • Hubs: Basic devices that connect multiple computers in a network (less common today).
  • Modems: Connect networks to the internet.
  • Cables & Wireless Signals: Used for physical and wireless connections.
  • Network Interface Cards (NICs): Allow computers to join a network.

Network Addressing

Every device on a network needs an address to send and receive data:

  • IP Address: A unique number (like 192.168.0.1) for each device
  • MAC Address: A number tied to the device's hardware
  • DNS: Changes website names (like google.com) into IP addresses

Benefits of Networking

Networks provide many advantages for users and organizations:

  • Share files, printers, and internet
  • Communicate easily through email or chat
  • Keep data safe with backups
  • Work from anywhere
  • Save money by sharing resources

Types of Networks

Different network types serve different needs and scales:

  • LAN: Small network for homes or schools
  • WAN: Large network over big distances (like the internet)
  • MAN: Covers a city or campus
  • PAN: Connects personal devices (like phones and watches)
  • WLAN: A wireless LAN using Wi-Fi

Wired Network

A wired network connects devices using physical cables (usually Ethernet) to transmit data. It's commonly used in homes, schools, and businesses for stable and secure connections.

Hardware Needed

  • Ethernet cables (Cat5e, Cat6, etc.) – used to connect devices.
  • Router – distributes the internet connection.
  • Switch (optional) – expands the number of wired connections available.
  • Network Interface Card (NIC) – built into most computers, allows connection via cable.
  • Modem – connects the network to the internet (often combined with the router).

Pros of Wired Networks

  • ✅ Faster speeds – typically more stable and faster than wireless.
  • ✅ Better security – harder to hack than Wi-Fi.
  • ✅ Reliable connection – less interference or dropouts.
  • ✅ Lower latency – good for gaming and video calls.

Cons of Wired Networks

  • ❌ Less mobility – devices are limited by cable length.
  • ❌ Installation can be messy – wires everywhere if not managed well.
  • ❌ More hardware needed – compared to wireless.
  • ❌ Harder to expand – not as flexible as Wi-Fi when adding new devices.
