Networking Overview

Computer networking is how devices—like computers, phones, and servers—connect and communicate with each other to share data and resources. Understanding networks is essential in today's digital world. Below is a quick look at the key parts of networking:

Networking Components

Networks are made up of several key components that help devices connect and communicate:

  • Routers: Direct data between different networks.
  • Switches: Connect devices within a network and manage traffic.
  • Hubs: Basic devices that connect multiple computers in a network (less common today).
  • Modems: Connect networks to the internet.
  • Cables & Wireless Signals: Used for physical and wireless connections.
  • Network Interface Cards (NICs): Allow computers to join a network.
